The Perturbation Toolbox

We trained 14 LoRA perturbation directions on a frozen language model. Each one steers generation toward a different narrative style. Here's what happens when you apply them to 12 different story openings.

DreamerML v3 · TinyStories-1M (1M params, GPTNeo) · All outputs deterministic (seed=42, temp=0.9, top_p=0.95) · 49,440 trainable parameters · 25 seconds training on one GPU

The Directions

Each direction is a rank-1 weight perturbation applied to the attention and MLP projections of layers 2–5. Weights are modified, text is generated, then everything reverts. The base model is never permanently changed. The perturbation scale (α=2.0) was calibrated to modify target weights by 8–15% of their original norm — enough to shift style without breaking coherence.

After GRPO training, the policy zeroed 2 of 16 directions and kept 14. Ranked by effect strength (KL divergence from base):

Name Patterns

One of the most striking findings: character names are not random. They cluster by perturbation direction.

The name shift happens because names in TinyStories are not uniformly distributed — they correlate with story style. "Lily" is the most common name in the training data for park/backyard stories. "Molly" clusters with emotional narratives. "Jane" appears more in outdoor/nature stories. The perturbation directions discovered these correlations without being told.

How It Works

The system has three learned components, totaling 49,440 parameters:

ComponentWhat it isParameters
LoRA basis16 rank-1 directions on layers 2–5 (out_proj + c_proj)28,672
Policy MLP16→128→128→16, tanh output, Gaussian noise20,768
Base modelTinyStories-1M (GPTNeo), frozen0 (frozen)

Training: 400 steps of GRPO with G=12 candidate actions per step. The reward is a Gaussian sweet-spot function: R = exp(-(KL - 0.5)² / 2σ²). This encourages perturbations that change the output distribution enough to be interesting (KL ≈ 0.5) without destroying coherence. A collapse penalty subtracts when entropy drops below 0.5 at any token position.

Total training time: 25 seconds on one GPU. The perturbation scale α=2.0 was calibrated against TinyStories-1M's weight norms (1.0–2.0), producing 8–15% modification at full strength.

All outputs above used seed=42, temperature=0.9, top_p=0.95. The only variable between columns is which rank-1 perturbation direction is active. Every output is deterministically reproducible.
